# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local service business owners (roofing, plumbing, HVAC, dental, cleaning, landscaping, etc.) who are losing leads daily because they can't answer every call or follow up fast enough. They arrive via three channels: cold outreach, referrals from other business owners, or QR codes handed to them directly. They're skeptical but curious. They want proof, not promises.

## Product Purpose

NTNS sells a complete AI-powered growth system for local businesses. Not just a voice agent: it's a 24/7 AI receptionist + instant follow-up + lead reactivation + automated review collection + full CRM (GoHighLevel), all installed as one system. The website's job is to make the visitor experience the product firsthand (live phone demo, upcoming video walkthrough) and understand the full scope of what gets built for them. Success: the visitor calls the demo number or reaches out to get started.

## Brand Personality

Direct, confident, relentless. NTNS doesn't sugarcoat. It tells business owners they're bleeding money, then shows them exactly how to stop. The tone is peer-to-peer (business owner to business owner), not corporate. No fluff, no jargon. Say it straight, prove it live.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with stock photos and vague value propositions
- Overly polished enterprise sites that feel corporate and distant
- AI company pages that lead with technology instead of outcomes
- Cluttered pages with too many CTAs competing for attention
- Anything that feels like it was made for VCs instead of the person reading it

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** The live demo IS the pitch. Every section should push toward experiencing the product, not just reading about it.
2. **Proof over promises.** Stats, live calls, system demos. If you can't prove it on the page, don't claim it.
3. **Respect the visit.** These are busy people who came from a cold call or a QR code scan. Get to the point fast. No filler sections.
4. **Full picture, one scroll.** They need to see everything they're getting (AI agent, follow-up, reactivation, reviews, CRM) in one clear flow, not buried across tabs.
5. **Peer confidence.** Talk like a business owner who solved this problem, not like a tech company selling software.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Reduced motion support (already present). Sufficient color contrast on all text. Touch targets sized for mobile (these users may scan a QR code on their phone).
